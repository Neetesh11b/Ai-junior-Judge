SUBJECT: Wrongful denial of Provident Fund withdrawal claim
         and arbitrary cancellation of EPF account No. DL/47821/003421

---------------------------------------------------------
BACKGROUND AND FACTS OF THE CASE:
---------------------------------------------------------

1. The Petitioner, Rajesh Kumar Verma, aged 58 years, was employed
   as a Senior Accounts Manager at M/s Sunrise Textiles Pvt. Ltd.,
   Okhla Industrial Area, New Delhi from 01 April 1991 to 31 March 2023,
   completing a total service period of 32 years.

2. During his entire tenure of employment, the Petitioner and his employer
   made regular monthly contributions to the Employees Provident Fund
   as mandated under the Employees Provident Funds and Miscellaneous
   Provisions Act, 1952. The total accumulated corpus in the EPF account
   of the Petitioner as on 31 March 2023 stood at Rs. 18,42,650/- (Rupees
   Eighteen Lakhs Forty-Two Thousand Six Hundred and Fifty only).

3. Upon retirement on 31 March 2023, the Petitioner submitted Form 19
   (Final PF Settlement) and Form 10C (Pension Withdrawal Benefit) on
   15 April 2023 through the EPFO Unified Member Portal for withdrawal
   of his entire provident fund accumulations.

4. Despite the submission of complete documentation, the Respondent No. 2
   (EPFO) rejected the withdrawal claim vide rejection letter dated
   20 June 2023, citing "discrepancy in KYC documents" without specifying
   the exact nature of the discrepancy. No opportunity of hearing was
   provided to the Petitioner before such rejection.

5. The Petitioner made multiple representations to the Regional PF
   Commissioner, Delhi (East) on 10 July 2023, 25 July 2023, and
   15 August 2023, requesting clarification and rectification.
   All representations went unacknowledged and unanswered.

6. Subsequently, the Petitioner discovered through RTI Application
   No. EPFOG/R/2023/50421 filed on 01 September 2023 that his EPF
   account had been arbitrarily tagged as "Inoperative/Suspicious"
   without any prior notice or show cause proceeding.

7. The Petitioner's family is in financial distress as he is the sole
   earning member and his wife requires continuous medical treatment
   for chronic kidney disease, incurring monthly expenses of Rs. 45,000/-.

---------------------------------------------------------
LEGAL ISSUES RAISED:
---------------------------------------------------------

I.  Whether the arbitrary rejection of EPF withdrawal claim without
    specifying reasons violates the principles of natural justice and
    Articles 14 and 21 of the Constitution of India?

II. Whether the tagging of the EPF account as "Inoperative/Suspicious"
    without issuing a Show Cause Notice violates Section 7A of the
    Employees Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952?

III. Whether the prolonged non-response to representations by the
     EPFO authorities amounts to a violation of the Right to Livelihood
     under Article 21 of the Constitution?

IV. Whether the Petitioner is entitled to interest on delayed payment
    at the rate of 12% per annum from the date of application?

---------------------------------------------------------
PETITIONER'S ARGUMENTS:
---------------------------------------------------------

1. VIOLATION OF NATURAL JUSTICE:
   The Respondents rejected the claim without providing any specific
   reason or opportunity of being heard, which is a clear violation of
   the audi alteram partem principle. The Hon'ble Supreme Court in
   Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India (1978) 1 SCC 248 held that any
   action affecting a citizen's rights must follow due process.

2. FUNDAMENTAL RIGHT TO LIVELIHOOD:
   The accumulated PF amount of Rs. 18,42,650/- represents 32 years
   of the Petitioner's hard work and savings. Denial of this amount
   directly violates his right to livelihood under Article 21.
   Ref: Olga Tellis v. Bombay Municipal Corporation (1985) 3 SCC 545.

3. ARBITRARY ADMINISTRATIVE ACTION:
   The tagging of account as "Suspicious" without any show cause notice
   or inquiry proceedings is ex-facie arbitrary and violates Article 14.
   The action is unreasonable and not backed by any valid reason or evidence.

4. MEDICAL EMERGENCY:
   The Petitioner's wife is suffering from chronic kidney disease requiring
   dialysis twice a week. The denial of PF funds is causing grave hardship
   and medical emergency to the family.

5. STATUTORY OBLIGATION:
   Under Para 72 of the EPF Scheme, 1952, the EPFO is bound to settle
   claims within 20 days of receipt of complete application. The Respondents
   have violated this statutory timeline by over 15 months.

---------------------------------------------------------
RESPONDENTS' ARGUMENTS:
---------------------------------------------------------

1. PENDING KYC VERIFICATION:
   The Respondents submitted that the withdrawal claim was put on hold
   due to mismatch between Aadhaar-linked name and EPF records. The name
   appears as "Rajesh Kumar" in Aadhaar vs "Rajesh Kumar Verma" in EPF
   records, which requires verification as per EPFO Circular dated 12.03.2022.

2. ANTI-FRAUD MEASURES:
   The account was flagged under EPFO's fraud detection algorithm due to
   multiple failed login attempts from different IP addresses, which is
   a standard security protocol to protect members' interests.

3. ADEQUATE REMEDY AVAILABLE:
   The Respondents argued that the Petitioner has an alternative remedy
   of filing a complaint before the EPF Appellate Tribunal and therefore
   the writ petition is not maintainable.

4. PROCESS COMPLIANCE:
   The rejection letter was sent to the registered address and mobile
   number on record. If the Petitioner did not receive it, the fault
   lies with the Petitioner for not updating his contact details.
